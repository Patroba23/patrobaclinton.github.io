I am Wanyama Clinton Patroba, an Urban and Regional Planner and GIS professional holding a Bachelor of the Built Environment in Urban and Regional Planning (Second Class Honours, Upper Division) from the Technical University of Kenya.

My work spans spatial data analysis, land use planning, environmental assessment, and transportation planning. I have applied GIS tools across real-world projects — mapping informal settlements in Nairobi's Mathare and Kibera, preparing a mixed-use masterplan for a university community in Kajiado County, and using GIS-based field planning at BURN Manufacturing.

I have hands-on experience working with government agencies, planning firms, traffic modelling companies, and research institutions — giving me a broad understanding of how spatial data drives decisions in urban development, transport, and environmental management.

I am currently expanding into GEO — Generative Engine Optimisation — exploring how AI-powered search and content visibility strategies can amplify geospatial work and urban planning knowledge in the digital space.

// AI-Powered Visibility

Generative Engine Optimisation

What is GEO?

Generative Engine Optimisation (GEO) is an emerging field focused on making content and expertise visible within AI-powered search engines — Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT Search, and Microsoft Copilot. Unlike traditional SEO, GEO focuses on how your knowledge and authority are surfaced and cited by AI systems when people ask questions. As a GIS professional, I recognised that geospatial knowledge — particularly for African urban contexts — is deeply underrepresented in AI-generated responses. I am now learning how to close that gap.
