GIS Portfolio

Informal Settlement Mapping – Nairobi City

As part of a research team, I used Geographic Information Systems (GIS) to map informal settlements across Nairobi, including Mathare, Kibera, Kahawa Soweto, and Mukuru kwa Njenga. The work involved spatial analysis of each settlement's unique socio-environmental characteristics, supported by field data collection, survey design, and data cleaning. Findings were visualised through maps and Excel dashboards to communicate patterns to researchers and stakeholders.

Tools used: ArcGIS · QGIS · Excel · Field Surveys

I also created presentations and visual materials using ArcGIS to communicate the master plan's concept to university staff, local authorities, and other stakeholders.

GEO — Generative Engine Optimisation

What is GEO and Why I'm Learning It

Generative Engine Optimisation (GEO) is an emerging field focused on making content and expertise visible and credible within AI-powered search engines such as Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT search, and Microsoft Copilot. Unlike traditional SEO, which targets web rankings, GEO focuses on how your knowledge, work, and authority are surfaced and cited by AI systems when people ask questions.

As a GIS and Urban Planning professional, I recognised that geospatial knowledge, research, and planning work is underrepresented in AI-generated responses — particularly for African urban contexts. I am now learning how to close that gap by building a credible, AI-readable digital presence around my expertise.

Currently studying: GEO fundamentals · AI search behaviour · Content authority building · Structured digital presence

GEO + GIS: My Planned Direction

My goal is to combine my GIS and Urban Planning background with GEO strategies to achieve two things. First, to make my own portfolio and projects more discoverable and citable by AI search engines. Second, to eventually help planning organisations, research institutions, and local government bodies in Kenya and across Africa build stronger digital authority so their work gets recognised in AI-generated answers about African cities, land use, and urban development.
